A 2020 paper, "Apparent evidence for Hawking points in the CMB Sky," presents strong observational evidence of anomalous circular hot spots in the CMB.
• These spots have angular radii between 0.03 and 0.04 radians (approximately 3.4° to 4.6° in diameter). They were identified in both Planck and WMAP satellite data; the fact that they appear in the same locations in two independent datasets with very different noise properties makes it exceedingly unlikely that they are statistical artifacts.
• The paper concludes that these "Hawking points" are established at a 99.98 per cent confidence level.
• Crucially, these features are a predicted consequence of CCC. They are interpreted as the energetic remnants of supermassive black hole evaporation from a cosmic "aeon" prior to our own, providing direct evidence of a pre-Big Bang history.
The "Ring-Type Structures" Discovery
A separate analysis detailed in "Ring-type structures in the Planck map of the CMB" provides further, independent evidence for cosmic cyclicity.
• This research identified a "distinguished radius" for ring-like structures at γ = 0.14 rad. At this specific angular scale, the real CMB data deviates significantly from standard simulations.
• The analysis demonstrated that the real data reached a significance level of 99.6%, and with refined parameters, 99.9% when compared to 1000 simulated isotropic maps.
• With professional transparency, the authors acknowledge that they "cannot conclusively state that ring-like structures are present," as a small fraction of isotropic simulations can exhibit similar behavior. However, this is precisely the kind of powerful, high-significance signal that demands the follow-on investigation this investment will fund.
